# Combat Screen

## Purpose

The combat screen is the heart of the game.

Players will spend most of their time here.

Its purpose is not only to present gameplay, but to immerse the player inside a living fantasy kitchen where every battle feels exciting, readable and satisfying.

The screen should never feel like a traditional mobile game interface.

It should feel like looking into a miniature world.

---

# Core Fantasy

The player is not controlling heroes through menus.

The player is standing above an enchanted preparation table while a brigade of adventurer cooks prepares an impossible fantasy meal.

Every chain on the puzzle board gives the brigade new opportunities to overwhelm the monster.

Cooking is part of the world's identity.

Combat remains combat.

The cooking fantasy exists to amplify the spectacle, not to change the gameplay.

---

# Design Philosophy

Gameplay readability is sacred.

Nothing is allowed to reduce the player's ability to:

- analyze the board
- discover long chains
- identify special tiles
- predict chain reactions

Every artistic decision must improve immersion without reducing clarity.

Whenever readability and decoration conflict, readability always wins.

---

# Player Eye Flow

The screen should naturally guide the player's attention.

Idle state:

1. Monster
2. Puzzle Board
3. Heroes

During gameplay:

Puzzle
↓

Heroes

↓

Monster

↓

Puzzle

The player's eyes should naturally loop between these three areas.

The UI should never interrupt this movement.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

The Monster is the emotional focus.

The Puzzle Board is the interaction focus.

The Heroes are the personality focus.

Everything else is secondary.

---

# The Puzzle Board

The puzzle board is not a widget.

The puzzle board is a physical object.

Imagine an enchanted chopping board placed on a giant preparation table.

It belongs to the world.

It has:

- wood grain
- knife marks
- flour dust
- subtle wear
- thickness
- depth

The board should feel satisfying to touch.

The board occupies roughly half of the screen because it is the player's primary interaction surface.

---

# The Heroes

Heroes are always alive.

Even when the player is thinking.

Avoid static idle poses.

Each hero should constantly perform small cooking-related activities.

Examples:

The Butcher sharpens a knife.

The Hunter sorts herbs.

The Mage stirs a magical pot.

The Gatherer prepares vegetables.

When a chain resolves, these idle actions naturally transform into combat actions.

The player should feel that the entire brigade works together.

---

# The Monster

The monster dominates the upper part of the screen.

It should be significantly larger than the heroes.

It reacts to every attack.

It should feel dangerous.

But also strangely appetizing.

Every monster should make the player wonder:

"What recipe could I make with this?"

---

# Living Environment

The combat does not happen inside a UI.

It happens inside a fantasy kitchen.

The environment should constantly reinforce this feeling.

Examples:

- hanging herbs
- lanterns
- simmering pots
- wooden shelves
- recipe notes
- spice jars
- smoke
- warm light
- baskets
- cooking tools

These elements should remain in the background and never reduce gameplay readability.

---

# User Interface

UI should disappear into the world.

Avoid floating panels.

Avoid generic RPG windows.

Whenever possible, UI elements should become physical objects.

Examples:

Health bars become engraved wooden signs.

Recipe notifications appear on handwritten recipe cards.

Combo counters hang from hooks or ropes.

Buttons look like handcrafted kitchen tools.

Everything should feel physically placed inside the environment.

---

# Feedback

Every player action should generate satisfying feedback.

Small chains should already feel rewarding.

Large chains should become spectacular.

Feedback layers include:

- animation
- particles
- sound
- character reactions
- monster reactions
- camera movement
- lighting

Chain reactions should escalate naturally.

The player should constantly feel:

"I can do even better."

---

# Emotional Goals

The player should feel:

• Cozy

• Clever

• Hungry

• Powerful

• Curious

• Rewarded

The game should celebrate creativity rather than efficiency.

---

# Golden Rule

The player should never feel like they are playing inside a mobile application.

They should feel like they are interacting with a tiny handcrafted fantasy kitchen where incredible adventures happen.
