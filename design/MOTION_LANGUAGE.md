# Interaction Feel

## Purpose

This document defines how the game should feel in the player's hands.

Every interaction should feel satisfying.

Every action should acknowledge the player.

The game should constantly encourage experimentation and longer chains.

---

# Core Principle

The player should never wonder if the game understood their action.

Every interaction must immediately produce visible feedback.

Even the smallest interaction deserves acknowledgement.

---

# Dragging

Dragging is the most frequent interaction.

It must feel effortless.

Desired feeling:

"I am painting across the board."

Not:

"I am selecting objects."

The chain should appear alive.

It should flow naturally.

---

# Tile Selection

Each tile immediately reacts when selected.

Possible feedback:

- slight scale increase
- soft glow
- tiny bounce
- subtle sound
- ingredient sparkle

The player should feel each ingredient joining the recipe.

---

# Chain Growth

Every additional tile increases excitement.

Visual intensity should slowly build.

Long chains should naturally become more spectacular.

Never jump directly from "small" to "huge".

Escalate smoothly.

---

# Chain Validation

When the player releases:

Immediately communicate success.

Never delay validation.

The player should instantly know:

"I created a valid chain."

---

# Combo Escalation

Every combo level increases energy.

More particles.

More camera movement.

More sound layers.

More character reactions.

The player should anticipate the next explosion.

---

# Special Tiles

Special tiles should feel exciting before they are activated.

The player should immediately think:

"I want to include this."

Visual cues should naturally attract attention without becoming distracting.

---

# Character Feedback

Heroes constantly react.

Idle.

Ready.

Excited.

Celebrating.

Working.

They never appear frozen.

Small animations are preferred over large dramatic ones.

---

# Monster Feedback

Every successful action should receive a reaction.

Tiny chains:

Small hit reaction.

Huge chains:

Large stagger.

Explosions.

Smoke.

Cooking effects.

The player should feel that every action matters.

---

# Camera

The camera is subtle.

Never cinematic.

Use movement only to reinforce gameplay.

Examples:

Tiny shake.

Tiny zoom.

Tiny impact.

Always readable.

---

# Rhythm

Gameplay follows a breathing rhythm.

Think.

Drag.

Release.

Explosion.

Celebrate.

Calm.

Repeat.

Never rush every moment.

Contrast creates satisfaction.

---

# Reward Philosophy

The game constantly rewards the player.

Small chain:

Nice.

Long chain:

Amazing.

Huge combo:

Incredible.

The player should never feel punished for making a smaller chain.

Only rewarded more for making a better one.

---

# Golden Rule

Every interaction should make the player smile.

The player should constantly feel:

"I want to do another chain."
